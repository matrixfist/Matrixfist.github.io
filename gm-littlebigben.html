History

Known only as Little Big Ben — his given name was never recorded — he was a child of approximately nine or ten years old who appeared on the banks of the Thames in 1717 and defeated every adult challenger who stepped forward over the course of three years. Witnesses described him as unhurried, almost bored, as if the outcome was already settled before it began. He disappeared from record in 1720. The single loss on his record was to a dockworker who admitted he had distracted the boy with a coin.

Signature Technique

The Tower — complete stillness of the torso while the arm moved independently, as if built on a separate axis. Clockwork described his metronomic timing: he waited for an interval known only to himself, then struck at the exact same moment in every match. The Strike was instantaneous, giving no wind-up.
