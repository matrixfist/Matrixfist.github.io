History

Giriama Kenga is the most transparent Grand Master in recorded history — she publicly acknowledged each of her twelve losses by the name of the opponent who achieved it, saying each one made her better. A coastal East African trader and community elder, she believed the game was a form of conversation between bodies, and that losing was simply receiving information you had not yet understood.

Signature Technique

Patient Observation — a pre-match ritual of studying an opponent for up to thirty minutes before agreeing to play, reading posture, breathing, and hand preference. Forest Spirit was her mid-match stillness, described by observers as disappearing into herself. The Long Wait was her name for the moment she would deliberately slow, forcing her opponent to commit before she did.
