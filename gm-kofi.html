History

A griot-warrior of the Mali Empire, Kofi Diabate embedded the game within the musical and oral traditions of his culture, using it as a teaching tool for rhythm, patience, and presence. He is the only Grand Master known to have taught the game publicly and systematically. His four losses are the most of any Grand Master above rank three — each opponent is named in the oral records he left behind.

Signature Technique

The Pendulum — a rhythmic oscillation of arm position that created the illusion of multiple simultaneous grip attempts, described by contemporaries as appearing to have seven hands. The Drum Beat was his tempo technique: he established a rhythm with his opponent and then broke it at the moment of contact.
