History

A former amateur boxer from Harlem who discovered the game in a Brooklyn shipyard in 1945, Marcus Cole adapted his boxing footwork and body mechanics to the grip game in ways no one had attempted before. He was the first player to theorise the spatial relationship between stance width and grip range, documenting his findings in a notebook that circulated in New York athletic circles throughout the 1950s and 60s. He retired unannounced in 1971.

Signature Technique

The Reach — an extended arm position that maximised grip range while minimising exposure, adapted from his boxing jab. Street Geometry was his systematic analysis of body angles and how small shifts in stance opened or closed grip opportunities. The Freeze was a complete stop at the moment of highest tension that reset his opponent's timing entirely.
