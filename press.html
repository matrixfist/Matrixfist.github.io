The Story

Matrix Fist is a competitive hand game with a verified global ranking system. Two players stand side by side, eyes forward, and compete to grip the other player's arm — forearm, wrist, hand, or fingers — and hold for one second to score a point. First to ten wins.

The game has existed in documented form since 821 AD, when Persian player Soraya al-Zafar recorded 3,200 verified wins with no losses across the Silk Road trading routes. Seven Grand Masters have been confirmed across eight centuries. The Eighth has not been found.

Matrix Fist — the app — is a competitive ranking platform built for iOS. Players log wins, confirm matches with their opponents, earn ranks, challenge others in their country and age group, and build a permanent verified record. At Master rank (1,000 confirmed wins), a serialised, embroidered Master Patch ships free to your door. At Grand Master rank (2,500 wins), an invitation follows. The position has never been filled in the current century.

"I built this because I wanted a game that required two people to be physically present with each other. No screens between them. Just hands, tension, and a record that doesn't lie."
— Shannon Iyer, Founder, Creative Dissonance
The Game

Players stand shoulder to shoulder, facing opposite directions. Unused hand behind the back. Eyes always forward — never look at your opponent sideways. Blocking is open palms only. No fists. No striking. No pulling. Grip and hold for 1 second to score. First to 5 wins (under 12) or 10 wins (13+).

The game requires no equipment, no space, and no prior physical training. It has been played in courtyards, markets, docks, boxing gyms, ship decks, and subway platforms. It scales from children to elite athletes. Grip strength, reaction time, spatial awareness, and patience are all in play.

The Grand Masters — Seven Confirmed

Seven players across eight centuries have been verified as Grand Masters. Each achieved 2,500 or more confirmed wins. Each is documented in the Matrix Fist archive at matrixfist.com/legend with their full backstory, signature techniques, and win record.

The seven confirmed Grand Masters, in chronological order: Soraya al-Zafar (821 AD, Persia, 3,200 wins, 0 losses) — Giriama Kenga (917 AD, Mombasa, 2,774 wins, 12 losses) — Kofi Diabaté (1193, Mali Empire, 2,900 wins, 4 losses) — Velu Nair (1441, Indian Ocean, 2,100 wins, 8 losses) — Little Big Ben (1717, London, 1,920 wins, 1 loss) — Hanako Mori (1874, Osaka, 3,100 wins, 6 losses) — Marcus Cole (1945, New York City, 2,600 wins, 9 losses).

The Eighth Grand Master position remains open. The app is counting every win.
